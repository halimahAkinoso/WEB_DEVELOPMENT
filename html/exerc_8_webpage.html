About NCC

The Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC) is the independent regulatory authority for the telecommunications industry in Nigeria. The NCC was created under Decree number 75 by the Federal Military Government of Ibrahim Babangida in Nigeria on 24 November 1992. The NCC was charged with the responsibility of regulating the supply of telecommunications services and facilities, promoting competition, and setting performance standards for telephone services in Nigeria. The Decree has been abrogated and replaced with the Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC) 2003. Idris Olorunnimbe is the current Chairman of the Board of the Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC), appointed by President Bola Tinubu in August 2025, while Aminu Maida is the present head of (NCC) assumed office in 2023.

Key Responsibilities
  1. Regulation: The NCC creates an enabling environment for competition among telecommunications operators and sets performance standards for service delivery.
  2. Consumer Protection: The Commission has a Consumer Affairs Bureau that addresses consumer complaints and ensures that service providers adhere to quality standards.
  3. Infrastructure Protection: Recently, the NCC has emphasized the importance of protecting telecommunications infrastructure as critical national assets, collaborating with national security agencies to prevent disruptions in service.

Our available in-demand courses

AI Developers Course

This 6-month course equips learners with the skills to build AI-powered applications from end to end. Blending full-stack development with core Generative AI concepts, students will gain hands-on experience in creating user interfaces, APIs, and advanced systems using LLMs, RAG, and agentic architectures. The curriculum also integrates product thinking and startup principles, preparing students for careers in tech or entrepreneurship.

AI Engineering

This 6-month course prepares learners to become AI engineers with a deep focus on Large Language Models (LLMs). Combining foundational AIML knowledge with hands-on experience, students will explore Transformer models, embeddings, RAG pipelines, and agentic frameworks like LangGraph. Theyll also work with leading tools in the Generative AI ecosystem, including OpenAI, Hugging Face, Anthropic, and Ollama.
